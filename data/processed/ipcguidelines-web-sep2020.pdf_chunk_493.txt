In some cases, SSI may appear up to 90 days after surgery. These are operations 
involving surgical implants and these conditions are listed at the end of this chapter.
Superﬁ  cial incisional SSI
x Discharge of pus from the superﬁ  cial incision 
x Pain, tenderness, localized swelling, redness or heat 
x Positive culture from aseptically collected specimen
Deep incisional SSI 
Infection appears within 30 days* of the procedure or within one year if there is an 
implant or foreign body, such as a prosthetic heart valve or joint prosthesis.106
x Pus discharge from the deep incision (muscle and fascial layers) 
x Spontaneous dehiscence or “gaping” of wound 
x Fever >38 °C, localized pain or tenderness 
x Positive culture from aseptically collected specimen
