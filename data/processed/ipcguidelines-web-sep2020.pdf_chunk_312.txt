Outbreaks
Bacterial food poisoning (acute gastroenteritis) is an infection or intoxication 
manifested by abdominal pain and diarrhoea, with or without vomiting or fever. The onset of symptoms may range from less than one hour to more than 48 hours after eating contaminated food. Usually, large numbers of organisms actively growing in food are required to initiate symptoms of infection or intoxication. Water, milk, and solid foods are all vehicles for transmission.
