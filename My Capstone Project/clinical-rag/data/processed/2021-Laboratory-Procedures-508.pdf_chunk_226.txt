The usual site for pediatric venipunctures is the antecubital fossa. Assess the size of the vein and use 
the appropriate needle size for a successful venipuncture. For difficult to find veins, use the Veinlite as described in Section 3.12 to locate a suitable vein and reassure the SP and the parent. Follow the general guidelines in Section 3.9 for performing venipunctures.
