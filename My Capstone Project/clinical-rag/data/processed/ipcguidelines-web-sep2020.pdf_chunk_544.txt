4. Construct a case deﬁ  nition
This is one of the most important steps in identifying the cases in outbreak 
investigation. Before counting cases, the investigating team must decide what to count, that is, what to call a case by using a set of standard criteria for classifying whether a person has a particular disease, syndrome, or other health condition called as case deﬁ  nition. Some case deﬁ  nitions, particularly those used for national 
surveillance, have been developed and adopted as national standards that ensure comparability. Using an agreed upon standard case deﬁ  nition ensures that every 
case is equivalent, regardless of when or where it occurred, or who identiﬁ  ed it.
