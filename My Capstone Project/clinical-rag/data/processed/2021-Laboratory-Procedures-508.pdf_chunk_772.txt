9.4.2 Components of Test Records  
Test records include: 
• Person performing the test (may be identified by initials or a unique 4-di git code);  
• Printed or electronically stored output from instrumentation that the technical 
supervisor judges as necessary to document the analytical result;  
• Date of specimen analysis;

• Date and time that specimen was collected; and  
• Condition of specimens that did not meet criteria for acceptable specimens 
(e.g., hemolyzed or insufficient quantity for test). 
Test records are maintained for a minimum of 7 years. Records may be bound in laboratory 
notebooks, bound in loose- leaf form, or stored electronically.
