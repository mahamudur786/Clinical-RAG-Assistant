Ventilator-associated pneumonia
Pneumonia is the second most common HAI reported in the world and is 
associated with substantial morbidity and mortality. Most patients with healthcare-associated pneumonia are those at the extremes of age, with severe underlying disease, immunosuppression, depressed sensorium and cardiopulmonary disease, and those who have had thoraco-abdominal surgery. Most bacterial healthcare-associated pneumonia occurs by aspiration of bacteria colonizing the oropharynx or upper gastrointestinal tract of the patient.
