Provide detailed explanations and inform them that the best technique is being used. It is very 
important that the adolescent maintain control of the situation; therefore, give them some choices, as well as allowing  them to help if offered. Be very clear about rules, such as remaining motionless, 
but do not restrain the adolescent. 
Pediatric venipunctures are most successful when children are immobile, the veins are maximally 
distended, and all supplies are handy. Many children prefer a cloth or gauze under the tourniquet to prevent pinching of skin.
