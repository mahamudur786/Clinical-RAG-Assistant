Posit
ive R esult . A specimen that produces two distinct red lines is positive for the presence of 
hCG. One line should be in the control region (C) and another line should be in the test region (T). 
Negative R esult . A specimen that produces a red line in the control region (C) and no apparent 
red or pink line in the test region (T) is negative for the presence of hCG.  
Invalid R esult (any of the following). For the test to be valid, a red line must appear in the 
control region (C). Insufficient sample volume or incorrect procedural techniques are the most 
likely reasons for control line failure. Review the procedure and repeat the test with a new test
