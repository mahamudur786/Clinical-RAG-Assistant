Healthcare-associated infections (HAIs) are neither present nor incubating at the 
time of admission of a patient to the healthcare facility (HCF). The majority of HAIs manifest after 48 hours of admission.
HAIs should be identiﬁ  ed on the basis of both clinical as well as laboratory criteria. 
Infection acquired in the hospital but not evident until after discharge is also considered as HAI. Infection in a newborn in a health facility may also be considered as an HAI.
